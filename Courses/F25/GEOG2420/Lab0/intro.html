Introduction

There are numerous commercial remote sensing software platforms available, each with their own strengths and weaknesses. Some commonly used software include ENVI-IDL, ERDAS-Imagine and PCI Geomatica. Because much of the work we will be doing in the labs is centred around viewing and interpreting imagery, we will not require the advanced toolkits packaged with the commercial software previously mentioned. Instead, in this course, we will be making use of the free and open source QGIS software package. While we will touch on some analytical methods used in remote sensing, QGIS, along with some of its common analytical plugins, has the tools necessary for carrying out these performing remote sensing data processing tasks.

An important part of the labs will be displaying and interpreting your results. As you will see, scale plays a critical role in this work. In order to do this effectively with remote sensing imagery, we will need to first review some basic cartographic principles. In this lab, you will follow the tutorial at this link. While this tutorial will give you the chance to get used to QGIS map making user interface, we will not require such advanced maps in subsequent labs. In general, I'd like to see maps with scalebars, titles, author and date, as well as colour bars if applicable for your submitted lab reports. Any additional specifications will be included in the lab assignments.

You do not need to submit a report for this lab, and it will not be graded.
